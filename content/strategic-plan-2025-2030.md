# RNADW Strategic Plan 2025-2030

**Theme:** "Loud in Silence: Deaf Women and Girls Driving Change"
**Tagline:** Participation ~ Power ~ Partnership!
**Period:** 1st January 2025 to 31st December 2030

---

## About RNADW

### Who We Are

We are The Rwanda National Association of Deaf Women (RNADW), an Organization of People with Disabilities (OPD) registered with Rwanda Governance Board (RGB) under Registration N° 055/2014.

We are a Deaf constituency-led Rwandan Sign Language users organization that exists to empower Deaf Women and Girls. RNADW was founded in 2005 by a group of Deaf Women Human Rights Activists to advocate for the rights of deaf women and girls, after realizing gaps in service provision and insufficient advocacy efforts to fight for their rights. We fight for full and meaningful social integration of deaf women and girls in the entire Rwandan society.

---

## Vision

**"An inclusive and just society where Deaf Women and Girls fully enjoy their rights, participate in decision-making processes and benefit from all aspects of life."**

---

## Mission

**To defend the rights of Deaf Women and Girls and promote their health rights, human rights, education and socio-economic justice.**

---

## Institutional Objectives

As per the RNADW Statute, the Institutional Objectives are:

1. **To protect Deaf Women and Girls rights and fight against any forms of violence against them.**
2. **To improve the wellbeing of Deaf Women and Girls and promote their full participation in Rwanda's development processes.**

## Our Target Communities

### Primary Communities

RNADW is primarily responsible to the following Communities of Deaf Women and Girls:

- **Deaf Women** 19 years and above with diverse sexual orientations, gender identity, educational and social backgrounds
- **Deaf Girls** 12-18 years - Adolescent and Young Girls (AYG) and First Time Teen Young Mothers (FTYM)
- **Deaf Children** aged 0-11 years
- **Deaf Women and Girls Refugees and IDPs**

### Secondary Communities

We strategically work with:

- **Parents and Care Givers of Deaf Children** - These provide strong familial support
- **Service Providers** - Sign Language Interpreters, Teachers, Healthcare Workers, Psychosocial Support/Counsellors, Legal Service Providers and Humanitarian Aid Workers
- **Children of Deaf Women (CODA)** - Children of deaf women linked to our primary communities

## Strategic Plan 2025-2030

### Overall Strategic Goal

**Foster inclusive social-economic development that is deaf-women-centred, transformative and accountable.**

### The 4 Strategic Pillars & 1 Institutional Development Priority

#### Strategic Pillar 1: My Body, My Rights
**Sexual and Reproductive Health and Rights (SRHR)**

**Goal:** Enabling legal, policy, social, economic and cultural environment that promotes, respects and guarantees sexual and reproductive health and rights for deaf women and girls.

**Objective:** To promote access to and full enjoyment of sexual and reproductive health and rights (SRHR) for deaf women and girls.

**Long-Term Outcome:** Deaf women and girls fully enjoy their SRHR and are free from all forms of GBV.

**Key Focus Areas:**
- Generate Evidence & Build Knowledge
- Empower Right-Holders
- Influence Systems & Service Providers
- Mobilise Communities & Partnerships

**Strategies:**
- SRHR Research, Documentation and Knowledge Management
- Legal and Policy Advocacy
- Capacity Building on SRHR
- GBV Prevention and Response
- Comprehensive Sexuality Education (CSE)
- Menstrual Health and Hygiene
- Deaf Theatre for SRHR Empowerment

---

#### Strategic Pillar 2: Spear N' Shield
**Education, Skilling, Digitalization and Business Development**

**Goal:** Education, skills, knowledge and digitalization competencies of deaf women and girls built and sustained.

**Objective:** To enhance the skills, abilities, knowledge and digitalisation competences of deaf women and girls and equip them with tools for meaningful engagement in Rwanda's social-economic-development agenda.

**Long-Term Outcome:** A diverse empowered pool of strong, resilient, productive, transformative and digitally empowered deaf women and girls built and sustained.

**Key Focus Areas:**
- Break Communication Barriers
- Ensure Inclusive Education
- Foster Economic Empowerment
- Leverage Digital Tools

**Strategies:**
- Promote and digitalize Rwanda Sign Language (RSL)
- Advocate for educational inclusion at all levels (primary to university, TVET)
- Skills training and business development support
- Access to capital (grants, SACCOs)
- Linkages with financial institutions and markets
- Umucyo Sign Language App development and deployment
- Digital literacy and ICT training

---

#### Strategic Pillar 3: Her Voice, Her Power
**Leadership, Voice and Agency**

**Goal:** Strengthened deaf women and girls voice, leadership and participation in all spheres of life.

**Objective:** To strengthen deaf women and girls' voice, leadership, meaningful participation and agency that fosters and drive systemic change to enhance sustainable linkages for joint advocacy and engagement.

**Long-Term Outcome:** Deaf women and girls championing disability inclusivity, gender equality, equity and social justice in their spheres of influence.

**Key Focus Areas:**
- Cultivate Leadership Pipelines
- Amplify Voices & Visibility
- Build Collective Power

**Strategies:**
- Feminist leadership institutes and boot camps
- Mentorship programs
- Media engagement and advocacy campaigns
- Public platform visibility
- Deaf Women Leaders Forum expansion
- Human rights advocacy training
- Participation in decision-making bodies

---

#### Strategic Pillar 4: Her Environment
**Climate Adaptation and Resilience**

**Goal:** Deaf Women and Girls mobilized to effectively influence and participate in national, regional and global climate justice agenda.

**Objective:** To mobilize Deaf Women and Girls and equip them with knowledge, skills, and attitudes on climate adaptation that will inspire them to influence and participate in social-economic-development opportunities.

**Long-Term Outcome:** A critical mass of environmentally engaged, resilient, and empowered deaf women and girls is built, sustained, and actively participating in environmental conservation and climate action.

**Key Focus Areas:**
- Mainstream Disability Inclusion in Climate Action
- Build Climate Resilience Capacities
- Ensure Accessible Climate Information

**Strategies:**
- Climate policy advocacy and integration
- Climate-smart agriculture training
- Sustainable livelihoods development
- Climate information in Rwanda Sign Language
- Early warning systems accessibility
- Disaster risk reduction training
- Environmental conservation participation

---

#### Institutional Development Priority: IDEAS
**Institutional Development, Engagement, Innovation and Sustainability**

**Goal:** RNADW nurtured into a center of excellence for deaf women's learning, innovation and programming.

**Objective:** To nurture RNADW's institutional capacity, financial independence, innovation, learning and operational efficiency to deliver on its mandate.

**Long-Term Outcome:** A vibrant, robust, resourced and sustainable women with disability rights organisation.

**Key Focus Areas:**
- Build a Foundation of Prudent Governance & Management
- Cultivate a Learning & Adaptive Organisation
- Drive Strategic Visibility & Influence
- Secure Sustainable Resources & Partnerships

**Strategies:**
- Robust governance systems and financial controls

- Skilled, diverse staff and board development
- MEAL (Monitoring, Evaluation, Accountability and Learning) systems
- Communications and branding strategy
- Resource mobilization and fundraising
- Strategic partnerships development
- Social enterprise models (SACCO)
- Digital innovation (Umucyo Sign Language App)
- Media platforms and ICT infrastructure

## Cross-Cutting Implementation Approaches

To achieve our strategic objectives, RNADW employs these core approaches across all program pillars:

- **Nothing About Us Without Us:** Ensuring meaningful participation of Deaf women and girls in design, implementation, monitoring, and evaluation
- **Sign Language Inclusivity:** All materials, meetings, and public engagements accessible through Rwanda Sign Language (RSL)
- **Community-Led Advocacy:** Building power and agency of members to advocate at community, district, and national levels
- **Strategic Partnership and Coalition Building:** Collaborating with OPDs, women's rights organisations, government agencies, and development partners
- **Digital Innovation:** Leveraging technology (Umucyo Sign Language App) to overcome communication barriers

## Theory of Change

### Envisioned Change

To achieve our Vision of "an inclusive and just society where Deaf Women and Girls fully enjoy their rights, participate in decision-making processes and benefit from all aspects of life", RNADW recognizes the need for a holistic and intersectional approach.

We will:
- Strengthen socioeconomic well-being through SRHR, education, and skills development
- Amplify voices through advocacy
- Enhance climate adaptation and resilience
- Expand inclusive education and employment opportunities
- Reinforce GBV prevention and response mechanisms
- Embed disability-inclusive strategies in climate resilience planning

### Pathways to Change

**Digital Innovation for Accessibility (Cross-Cutting)**
- Leverage technology, primarily through the Umucyo Sign Language App
- Overcome communication barriers
- Serve as central hub for remote sign language interpretation
- Accessible information dissemination and community building
- Ensure Deaf women and girls can directly access services, knowledge, and peer support
